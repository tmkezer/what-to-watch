Content Based Filtering

Content Based Filtering works by comparing different features from movies such as movies, actors, genre, directors, keywords, production company, and writers then those words are turned into numbers which is Count vectorization. Then by looking at those numbers the algroithim runs a cosine calculation inorder to find the smallest angles, which would become the most similar points.

Collabrative Filtering

The collabrative filtering model looks for similar users based on their ratings for certain movies. The top-N recommendation algorithm uses a similarity-based vector model to identify the k most similar users to an active user. After the k most similar users are found, their corresponding user-item matrices are aggregated to identify the set of items to be recommended. The algorithm baiscally finds a similarity between two users based on ratings that they gave on the specific movies.
